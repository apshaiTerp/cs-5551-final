What is all this? Why Board Games? And what's with the turtles anyway?

What is all this?

The Terrapin Game Collection Manager is a system being developed as part of the Advanced Software Engineering class offered at UMKC during the Spring Semester of 2015.

This project is being developed by Adam Carter. The main components of this project are this site, written primarily in HTML5 using Javascript and Bootstrap, supported by a set of REST services writing using the Spring platform running on Tomcat, and using a MongoDB database for storage.

This project brings together three of the things I am most passionate about, Computers, Games, and Terrapins. You can read more about those below.

Computer Nerd by Day

After graduating in 2004 from the University of Maryland - College Park, I have been working as a Software Engineer for Cerner Corporation, a Health Care IT company, where I am current a Software Architect. I am also currently working on my Masters of Computers Science at UMKC.

Gamer By Night

I have enjoyed Board Games all my life, but I have become an avid hobbyist and collector of Board Games over the past 4 years. I host regular Game Nights and enjoying spreading the hobby with anyone willing to come and play. With an ever growing game collection of my own, this seemed a great opportunity to solve a problem I, as well as many other hobbyists, routinely face: How do I keep track of what's in my collection?

And Terrapins... ?

Terrapins (which are acquatic turtles), have both an academic and personal meaning to me. I was introduced to the Terrapin as a student at the University of Maryland, which has the Terrapin as it's mascot. I later owned and raised Terrapins (up to 11 at one point) for nearly ten years. They are curious, friendly, and beautiful creatures. You can learn more about conservation efforts here.
